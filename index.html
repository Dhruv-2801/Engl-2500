Smit's Journey

Meet Smit Patel, a junior at Iowa State University majoring in Cyber Security. Born and raised in Ahmedabad, India, Smit's story is not just about survival it's about transformation, resilience, and the unwavering human spirit.

June 22, 2017

⚡ The Turning Point

During the final match at Khel Maha Kumbh, Smit was the captain of his high school basketball team. In the middle of the game, he suddenly started coughing blood. The match stopped immediately, and he was rushed to the hospital. This day changed everything.

July 2017

💔 The Diagnosis

After tests and reports, Smit was diagnosed with cancer. "Everything happened so fast," he recalls. "One day I was a normal student; the next, I was fighting for my life." The fear of uncertainty was harder than the illness itself.

2017-2019

🏥 The Battle

Chemotherapy took its toll. Smit lost his hair and gained weight, reaching 145 kilograms. "I couldn't even recognize myself in the mirror," he said. At school, classmates teased him about his appearance. The emotional pain sometimes hurt more than the treatment.

2019-2021

💪 The Transformation

Instead of giving up, Smit fought back. He turned to meditation, yoga, and basketball practice. Through consistent gym workouts and a strict diet, he transformed his body, losing 50kg in around 2 years. "Cancer taught me what real strength means — it's about refusing to give up."

April 2022

🎉 Cancer-Free

Smit was declared cancer-free. Feeling like he had been given a second chance, he decided to pursue his passion for Cyber Security and applied to universities in the United States.

2022-Present

🚀 New Beginnings

Smit was accepted by Iowa State University. "Coming to the U.S. was exciting but scary," he said. He joined the Cyber Defence Club, started research on 5G IoT attacks with Dr. Shivani Desai, and continues to balance academics, research, fitness, and social life.

Physical & Emotional Battles

During treatment, Smit faced severe physical changes and social isolation. Classmates teased him about his weight, and no one wanted to be his project partner. Research shows that children experiencing visible changes due to illness often face bullying and social isolation, which can significantly affect mental health and recovery. Despite these challenges, Smit refused to give up.

Cultural Adaptation

Moving to the United States brought new challenges. Smit faced culture shock, language barriers, and unfamiliar academic methods. "Sometimes my accent made people ask me to repeat things," he laughed. "But now I see that it's okay — my accent is part of who I am." Being multilingual (Hindi, Gujarati, and English) helped him navigate these challenges and form connections with other international students.

Vision for the Future

Big Dreams Ahead

Smit dreams of either starting his own cybersecurity company or joining a government cybersecurity team, contributing to protecting sensitive information on a national level. His experience with illness, discipline, and cross-cultural adaptation have shaped not only his technical abilities but also his determination to make a meaningful impact.

Beyond his career goals, Smit wants to inspire others facing illness, bullying, or cultural adjustment challenges. He wants to show them that with hard work, determination, and self-belief, adversity can be transformed into growth and success.

"Cancer took a lot from me, but it also taught me resilience, discipline, and the importance of pursuing my passion. Moving to the U.S. was another challenge, but it gave me opportunities I couldn't have imagined."

— Smit Patel

A Message of Hope

Today, Smit continues to balance academics, research, fitness, and social life — all while working toward his cybersecurity dreams. His story shows that perseverance, hard work, and self-belief can turn the darkest moments into powerful transformation.
